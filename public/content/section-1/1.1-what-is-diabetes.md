Type 1 Diabetes (T1D) is a chronic autoimmune condition where the body’s immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas.  

Insulin is a vital hormone that acts as a key. Insulin unlocks your body's cells so that glucose (sugar) from the food you eat can enter and be used for energy. Without insulin, glucose cannot enter the cells. Glucose builds up in the bloodstream instead, leading to high blood sugar levels, while the body's cells essentially starve for energy.  

Type 1 diabetes can develop at any age, though it is most frequently diagnosed in children, teens, and young adults. It is a lifelong condition that requires daily management.
